Footprints

Common parts:

  • Basic ceramic capacitor - 200-mil lead spacing, typically decoupling capacitors.
  • Basic resistor - 1/8 watt (maybe 1/4 also), vertical, 200-mil lead spacing. Should work for small signal diodes too.
  • Common Mounting hole - Just a simple shortcut to create a 400 mil diameter pad with a 130 mil drill hole that PCB will treat as a component (so it won't try to move it during autorouting).
  • Basic Transistor - 2N2222 or similar, standard TO-18 case, hand redrawn based on Eagle's part.

IC's:

  • Basic 4016/1066 Switch - Derived from standard DIL14 generic footprint.
  • 74LS74 - Common 74LS74 Flipflop, 14-DIL package.
  • 744040 - 12-bit Counter IC with Reset, Derived from generic DIL16 footprint.
  • MXD1000 - 4 to 500 ns Delay line by Maxim. Derived from generic DIL8 footprint.
  • CY7C138 - Dual-Port 4Kx8 SRAM by Cypress, derived from generic PLCC68 Socket footprint.
  • MOS 6581 SID chip - derived from generic DIL28 footprint, with proper pin names.

Connectors:

  • Female ATX connector - As used on most mainboards. 20-pin version, based on Tyco/AMP part #2-794954-0.
  • Female ATX, right angle - 20 pin version, based on Tyco/AMP part #2-1586041-0.
  • Female power jack - generic footprint, should work for most wall warts, Hand-redrawn from Eagle. UNTESTED.
  • Female 5-pin DIN - PCB mounted, suitable for older keyboards, etc. Hand-redrawn from Eagle. UNTESTED.
  • Female 15-pin D-Sub (short) - Shortened version suitable for use at the edge of a board, similar to PCB's other SubD connectors.
  • Female 15-pin D-Sub - Standard HD-15 SubD female VGA connector, converted from Eagle. UNTESTED.
  • Female 24-position Edge - Suitable for Commodore 64/128 "User Port" connector.
  • Female stereo jack - Hand-redrawn from Eagle, should match the one Radio Shack sells. UNTESTED.
  • Male 44-position Edge - Suitable for Commodore 64/128/etc usage. UNTESTED.
